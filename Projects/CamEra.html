CamEra

CamEra is a mobile app I developed using React Native, designed to simplify the process of selecting the perfect digital camera. In February 2024, I created this app as part of a project focused on enhancing the shopping experience for photography enthusiasts. CamEra features a user-friendly interface, which I designed in Figma, and showcases nine popular compact camera brands, allowing users to compare and find the best fit for their needs efficiently.

THE INSPIRATION

Throughout the CamEra project, I benefited from extensive resources that allowed me to shape a tailored solution for frequent social media users, particularly women-identifying individuals. Compact cameras have recently surged in popularity, appearing on platforms like Etsy and eBay, and I sought to simplify the research process for users. By gathering details like camera specifications, battery types, photo quality, and release dates, I aimed to create an informative and user-friendly experience.

Infusing the design with a feminine color palette, I wanted to appeal to my target audience by using softer, inviting tones. The goal was to help users build better spending habits by ensuring they make informed decisions and avoid impulsive purchases. This approach stands in contrast to a more generic shopping experience, offering users personalized recommendations based on thorough research.

The project was heavily front-end skill-focused, as I built the prototype from the ground up using React Native. This involved designing efficient component layouts and implementing responsive design principles, ensuring a seamless experience across various devices. Through this hands-on approach, I gained practical knowledge in front-end development, enhancing my ability to bridge the gap between design and functionality. Overall, the CamEra project was a transformative experience that deepened my understanding of user-centered design and the technical skills necessary to bring an idea to life.
